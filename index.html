Cognitive Load Index (CLI). Critical load → immediate cognitive adjustment recommended.

CLI is an experimental, non-diagnostic metric derived from time-on-task, perceived difficulty, and inefficiency signals.

Methodology & Assumptions

Definition. CLI is a bounded scalar (0–100) estimating short-term cognitive strain.

Per-task load:

  • Difficulty is self-rated on a 0–1 scale.
  • Time-on-task is normalized and capped at one hour.
  • Task load is the mean of difficulty and time exposure.

Assumptions.

  • Time-on-task contributes more reliably to fatigue than difficulty alone.
  • Users can approximately rank task difficulty.
  • Inefficiency signals reflect transient cognitive friction.
  • Cognitive load may accumulate without immediate subjective awareness.

Limitations.

  • CLI does not measure neural or physiological activity.
  • Environmental and emotional variables are not explicitly modeled.
  • Difficulty ratings are subjective and unstandardized.
  • The model is descriptive, not diagnostic.

Falsifiability.

  • CLI fails to increase during prolonged low-difficulty work.
  • High CLI consistently contradicts reported or behavioral fatigue.
  • Inefficiency signals show no correlation with overload.
